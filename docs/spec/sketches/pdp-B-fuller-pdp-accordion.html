Methylparaben In your avoid list

A preservative with mild estrogenic activity in lab studies. Banned in some EU contexts for leave-on products. You marked parabens in your avoid list.

Phenoxyethanol

A common preservative considered safer than parabens, but flagged for skin irritation at higher concentrations. Generally fine in rinse-off products.

PEG-40 Stearate

A surfactant whose safety depends on processing — can carry trace 1,4-dioxane contamination from manufacturing. Many brands now use cleaner alternatives.

Behentrimonium Methosulfate

A conditioning agent. Considered safer than older "quat" surfactants but still flagged by some clean databases for environmental persistence.

Glycerin

Plant-derived humectant that draws water into skin. One of the most studied and reliably safe moisturizing ingredients in personal care.

Ceramide NP

A naturally occurring lipid that helps restore the skin barrier. Lab-synthesized to mimic human ceramides — well tolerated.

Hyaluronic Acid

A water-binding polysaccharide naturally present in skin. Non-irritating across skin types.

Niacinamide

Vitamin B3 derivative that supports the skin barrier and helps regulate sebum. Strong safety data.

Panthenol

Pro-vitamin B5 — soothing, hydrating, low-allergen profile in clinical literature.

Cholesterol

A lipid component native to skin's outer layer. Pairs with ceramides to repair the barrier.

Ceramide AP

Another barrier-supporting ceramide variant. Same safety story as Ceramide NP.

Sodium Hyaluronate

The salt form of hyaluronic acid — smaller molecule, penetrates a bit better.

Water

The base solvent. Inert, ubiquitous, and not the part of the formula worth flagging.
