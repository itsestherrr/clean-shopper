Caution Facial Cleanser

A creamy, non-foaming face wash that removes dirt and makeup without disrupting the skin barrier. Most ingredients are skin-friendly, but two in your personal avoid list show up below.

Fragrance Avoid

"Fragrance" can cover dozens of unlisted compounds, often including known allergens and endocrine disruptors. You marked this in your avoid list.

Methylparaben Avoid

A preservative with mild estrogenic activity in lab studies. Banned in some EU contexts for leave-on products. You marked parabens in your avoid list.

Phenoxyethanol Caution

A common preservative considered safer than parabens, but flagged for skin irritation at higher concentrations. Generally fine in rinse-off products.

PEG-40 Stearate Caution

A surfactant whose safety depends on processing — can carry trace 1,4-dioxane contamination from manufacturing. Many brands now use cleaner alternatives.

Behentrimonium Methosulfate Caution

A conditioning agent. Considered safer than older "quat" surfactants but still flagged by some clean databases for environmental persistence.

Glycerin Clean

Plant-derived humectant that draws water into the skin. One of the most studied and reliably safe moisturizing ingredients in personal care.

Ceramide NP Clean

A naturally occurring lipid that helps restore the skin barrier. Lab-synthesized to mimic human ceramides — well tolerated and supported by dermatology research.

Hyaluronic Acid Clean

A water-binding polysaccharide naturally present in skin. Holds up to 1,000× its weight in water; non-irritating across skin types.

Niacinamide Clean

Vitamin B3 derivative that supports the skin barrier and helps regulate sebum. Strong safety data even at concentrations above what's typical here.

Panthenol Clean

Pro-vitamin B5 that converts to pantothenic acid in skin — soothing, hydrating, low-allergen profile in clinical literature.

Cholesterol Clean

A lipid component native to skin's outer layer. In formulations it pairs with ceramides and fatty acids to repair the barrier.

Ceramide AP Clean

Another barrier-supporting ceramide variant. Same safety story as Ceramide NP — well studied, well tolerated.

Sodium Hyaluronate Clean

The salt form of hyaluronic acid — smaller molecule, penetrates a bit better. Same safety profile as HA itself.

Water Clean

The base solvent. Inert, ubiquitous, and not the part of the formula worth flagging.
