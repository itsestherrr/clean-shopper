BD-01Decided
Three components, not one with variants
Badges in Clean Shopper carry three different jobs — status safety rating, category organization, and accent/premium moment. They share a shape-language family but have incompatible semantics. Split into three dedicated components so the role-lock is enforced at the type level.
StatusBadge · CategoryTag · AccentBadge
Each component owns one job. StatusBadge: clean/caution/avoid safety rating. CategoryTag: stone-tint org tag, non-judgmental. AccentBadge: lime premium moment on amethyst surfaces only.
The three roles
Clean Skincare Editor's pick
Three boundaries at the type level. Preserves role-lock: a dev can't accidentally ship a lime status badge or a semantic category tag. Each component has its own API, its own shape, its own rules.

My recommendation: Status = A (pill), Category = B (tight 4px), Accent = A (asymmetric). Rationale: Status gets the loudest shape because it carries safety meaning — pill is universally "badge-like" and readable at small sizes. Category gets the crispest, quietest shape (4px) so twelve category tags don't turn a listing page into a parade. Accent uses rounded-asym-sm so the lime premium moment echoes the card signature shape — ties the brand language together without reusing a shape already loaded with meaning elsewhere.
What to avoid: Category at pill (option C) would clash with StatusBadge-as-pill and blur the hierarchy. Accent as a pill (option B) reads like a button — bad, since badges aren't interactive. Accent as 16px soft (D) is safe but gives up the chance to do something signature.

PC-11Decided
Click target — modal or PDP?
Currently clicking a ProductCard opens a Modal overlay with product details. This changes — clicking navigates to a dedicated PDP (product detail page) route. Confirms the behavior + flags the downstream work.
A · Modal overlay (v3)
opens <Modal> — stays on route
Current. Fast to close. Can't link to a product. No browser back button.
B · PDP route (new)
navigates to /product/:id
Shareable URL. Back button works. Room for full ingredient breakdown.
Decision: B — PDP route. A product has enough information (ingredient safety breakdown, source citations, user notes, swap suggestions) that a modal can't hold it without scrolling-inside-scrolling. A route also makes products shareable and gives us a real URL to link to from shopping lists. Modal becomes reserved for transient actions (confirm, edit list, quick-save). Why not A: modals can't deep-link, can't be browser-back-navigated, and force a cramped layout. The v3 modal was a shortcut for "no route yet" — we're now past that.
Downstream work this implies:
  • Add /product/:id route
  • Build ProductDetailPage (new component)
  • ProductCard onClick → navigate, not open modal
  • Retire Modal usage in BrowsePage, SearchPage, LibraryPage for product detail
  • Decide: keep Modal component for other uses, or remove entirely?
